WALKING - A SIMPLE WAY TO KEEP FIT

Walking is good for your heart and lungs and helps improve cardiovascular fitness. It can be fitted into your daily routine and you can do it anywhere without any special equipment. Walking helps to prevent bone thinning that can lead to osteoporosis in later life. What’s more, you can still derive health benefits by taking short walks of ten to fifteen minutes, spread throughout the day, instead of waiting to have a long stretch of 30 minutes or more. Short walks can be built into the busiest of daily routines. You can try getting off the bus a few stops earlier or leaving the car at home and walking to the shops. At work, go for are juvenating walk in your lunch hour instead of sitting at your desk, and take the stairs instead of the lift. Best of all walking is free and at no cost to you. It is not age- restrictive and does not require any special gym equipment. All you need is a pair of comfortable canvas and you are ready to go read more...

Here are some reasons why you should start walking for fitness:

  • Walking strengthens your heart and helps reduce the risk of heart attack.
  • Walking improves physical and respiratory functions.
  • Walking is good for your brain and your bones.
  • Walking can help alleviate symptoms of depression.
  • Walking reduces the risk of some cancers such as colon cancer.
